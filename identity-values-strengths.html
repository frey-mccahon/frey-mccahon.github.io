FREY-TECH / CORE / IDENTITY, VALUES AND STRENGTHS

Identity, Values and Strengths


Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and the consequences of your action.

Racking my brain over and over again trying to find a good and relevant answer to this question had me bending scenarios and situation so far that I was conjuring up fictional ethical dilemmas. That gave birth to a new ethical dilemma, do I make up a fake story to fit the bill of the question at hand or do I offer up a benign but truthful situation. Well, that lead me here, to decide to offer my ethical dilemma of coming up with an ethical dilemma. How did I weigh this up as an appropriate choice? I think it gets right at the heart of the neurotic nature behind my ethical decision making. I often find it hard to drill into and hit a hard centre of a philosophical or ethical subject, often getting lost in sort of meta-questions.


Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

To answer this question I have to identify my culture. Ultimately I identify my culture as the things that shaped me the most, those things I believe are the popular culture that surrounded my childhood. Plastic consumer goods. The images, sounds and narratives that came out of the TV. The design language of advertisement and products. All these things have created an obsession with design and story telling in me. I believe these things aren’t as shallow as they often seem and dominate the shape of society’s spirit.


Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development

I believe a core strength of mine is an ability to identify compatibility between elements that seem conventionally unlinked. I think this ability is aided by an interest in finding common pitfalls in thought processing, one being the human instinct to reject contradiction in the desperate search for an answer to a question. Another pitfall that I try to safe guard against is the way momentum and narrative in the development of thoughts and ideas can hurry past the best solutions, to help aid this problem I like to use techniques like ‘beginner’s mind’. A limitation of mine would be a struggle with perfectionism.


Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they intersect with learning obstacles.

I believe the 'beginner's mind' technique is a powerful and versatile tool in many situations. It can be a life saver when you are feeling bogged down in confusion and feeling uninspired to find a new sort of solution. When you emulate the frame of mind of coming to an old problem as if it were the first time, you often find things you wouldn’t have seen if not for running that emulated frame of mind.


Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

I have had this experience many times. I believe that often the best way to elevate tension when it arises in a group setting is to momentarily pull back and give the atmosphere some time to change.
