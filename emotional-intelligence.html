Emotional Intelligence


What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is the one’s ability to recognise and understand and manage emotions. This might be one’s own emotional processes, or someone else’s, or the social atmosphere in a group setting.


How is it different from IQ?

IQ is about one’s understanding of logical processes, a subject well integrated into the familiar and tangible framework of applied technical activity. EQ is more esoteric, instead of building on a previously established knowledge base, it often involves letting go of preconceived ideas or narratives and using careful base instinct.


Why is it important?

EQ is nearer the base of human processes. It is tightly related to the foundation under the organised thought that frames concepts like IQ. It is often disregarded as unimportant to those pursuing processes optimised for productivity, but it is more important than people often realise, it can reframe perspective in fundamental ways that are usually hidden from the view of more IQ-aligned approaches.
