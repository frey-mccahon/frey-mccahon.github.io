Thinking like a programmer


What is your process so far for solving problems?


That is a hard question to answer because it can vary greatly depending on the why / what / when of the problem. Generally though I use a focus / defused gear switching approach to solving problems. If i'm not making traction with 'close focus' i will switch to a more relaxed 'defused' mode of thought and soon come back to 'close focus' again.


Introduce yourself


I’m Frey. I was born and have spent the vast majority of my life in Central Auckland. A value I try to cultivate is one of actively changing my modes of thought. Concepts like ‘beginner’s mind’ and ‘focused and diffused thinking’ are immensely valuable to me and have become integrated into my general patterns of thought. I’m broadly interesting in making things, recording ideas down and communicating ideas. Over the last few years more and more of these ideas have become related to software in one way or another, so I thought I would bite the bullet and learn about the subject. I’m enjoying the nature of it so far and am looking forward to going deeper!


What was it like trying to summarise yourself to a group of strangers?


It wasn't easy. I got a little hung up on it and probably over thought it.


Could you feel your ego? Were you self conscious?


yes and yes.


What parts of your learning strategy worked? What problems did you face?


Trying to stick to the timeboxing was a challenge to me. I had to fight the urge to get stuck down some rabbit hole. However i did become aware of this and became more dedicated to the timeboxing technique which worked in my favor. When i came across something i wanted to look into more deeply i made a note of it to research later.


Which parts of the work did you enjoy?


I enjoyed working within the terminal.


Which parts of the work did you find tedious?


I didn't find anyone thing particularly tedious, things only became tedious when i was in a bad frame of mind and needed a break.
